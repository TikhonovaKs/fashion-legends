Great Icons of the 90s Fashion, Their Style, Their Influence.

Welcome to the exhibition '90s Fashion Legends', a journey into an era when fashion runways transformed into a platform for a style revolution and women's independence. Here, you will encounter the greatest models of the 90s, their stories, their style, and how they shaped the era's image. Before you unfolds the grandeur and influence they bequeathed.

Step into the world of an iconic fashion era and feel the power that transformed the image of womanhood, paving the way to her independence. '90s Fashion Legends' is a guide through a realm of style, emancipation, and inspiration.

Kate Moss

URL

katemoss.com

In the 1990s, Kate Moss became a cult model thanks to her androgynous appearance and unique style. She collaborated with leading photographers and brands.

Kate Moss's unique appearance and androgynous style set her apart from the familiar standards of that time. She collaborated with outstanding photographers and designers such as Mario Sorrenti, Corinne Day, and Calvin Klein. Her work with them helped set new standards in fashion and photography. Kate Moss became a symbol of "heroin chic" in 90s fashion. Her demeanor and style became an icon of that era.

Cindy Crawford

URL

cindy.com

Cindy Crawford was recognized as one of the most beautiful women of her time. Her remarkable features, with thick hair and striking facial characteristics, immediately caught the attention of modeling agents.

Her career began in the early 80s, but in the 90s, her popularity skyrocketed. She was one of the leading figures of the "Big Five" supermodels of the 90s. Cindy Crawford collaborated with major global brands such as Versace, Chanel, and Revlon. Her face became recognizable thanks to advertising campaigns and covers of international magazines.

Naomi Campbell

URL

naomicampbell.com

Naomi Campbell drew attention with her extraordinary exotic beauty. Her black hair, expressive facial features, and elegant posture immediately captured attention.

In 1988, Naomi became the first black model whose face graced the cover of the French edition of Vogue. Linda Evangel

URL

levangelista.net

Linda Evangelista was known for her incredible ability to transform. She could adapt her appearance to various styles, making her particularly in demand for photo shoots.

Her ability to change her appearance inspired many photographers and designers. She was willing to experiment with different styles and looks. Linda was part of a movement that brought new standards to the fashion industry. She participated in the establishment of new modeling agencies and associations. She worked with numerous well-known brands such as Chanel, Dolce & Gabbana, and Versace. Her face graced the covers of many fashion magazines.

Claudia Schiffer

URL

claudiaschiffer.com

Claudia Schiffer became known for her striking blonde hair and classic European beauty. Her tall, statuesque figure and blue eyes made her a standout in the industry.

She had a long-standing collaboration with the iconic fashion house Chanel. Karl Lagerfeld, the creative director of Chanel, famously referred to her as his "Muse." Claudia was incredibly versatile, able to pull off a wide range of looks and styles. She was equally comfortable in high fashion editorials as well as commercial campaigns. Claudia Schiffer was one of the first models to truly achieve international supermodel status. Her face was everywhere, from magazine covers to billboards. Besides Chanel, Claudia worked closely with other renowned designers like Karl Lagerfeld, Gianni Versace, and Valentino.
